     twas the night before christmas   when all through the house   not a creature was stirring    not even a mouse    the stockings all hung   by the chimney with care   in hopes that st. nicholas   soon would be there    the children were nestled   all safe in their beds   while visions of sugarplums   danced in their heads    and mom in her kerchief   and i in my cap   had just settled down   for a long winters nap        when out on the lawn   there arose such a clatter   i sprang from my bed   to see what was the matter    away to the window   i flew like a flash   tore open the shutters   and threw up the sash    the moon on the breast   of the new fallen snow   gave the luster of midday   to object below    when, what to my wandering eyes   should appear   but a miniature sleigh   and eight tiny reindeer        with a little ol' driver   so lively and quick   i knew in a moment   it must be st. nick    more rapid than eagles   his courses they came   as he whistled and shouted   and called them by name   '
